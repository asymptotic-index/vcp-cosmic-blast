# Failure Modes
Cosmic Blast — Venus Cooling Project

---

## Purpose of This Document

This document enumerates the **primary failure modes** of the Cosmic Blast
concept. Each failure mode represents a way in which extreme, impulsive energy
release fails to produce cooling and instead worsens planetary conditions.

---

## Failure Mode 1 — Thermal Overkill

**Description:**  
Energy sufficient to eject atmosphere catastrophically overheats the planet.

**Cause:**  
- Escape-energy-scale deposition
- Rapid thermalization in dense atmosphere

**Effect:**  
- Global temperature spikes
- Surface and crustal heating
- Net warming dominates any mass loss

**Classification:**  
Fundamental physical failure

---

## Failure Mode 2 — Inefficient Atmospheric Escape

**Description:**  
Only a small fraction of heated gas actually escapes.

**Cause:**  
- Collisional energy redistribution
- Turbulent, stochastic outflow

**Effect:**  
- Most atmosphere remains bound
- Energy cost per kg escaped skyrockets

**Classification:**  
Momentum coupling failure

---

## Failure Mode 3 — Shock-Driven Greenhouse Amplification

**Description:**  
Shock heating increases atmospheric opacity and scale height.

**Cause:**  
- Compression heating
- Enhanced infrared absorption

**Effect:**  
- Stronger greenhouse trapping
- Slower post-event cooling

**Classification:**  
Feedback failure

---

## Failure Mode 4 — Surface Resurfacing

**Description:**  
Blast energy couples into the planetary surface.

**Cause:**  
- Pressure waves
- Crustal energy absorption

**Effect:**  
- Partial or global resurfacing
- Long-term heat retention

**Classification:**  
Structural damage failure

---

## Failure Mode 5 — One-Time Event Fallacy

**Description:**  
Assuming a single blast produces permanent change.

**Cause:**  
- Ignoring atmospheric rebound
- Ignoring solar energy input

**Effect:**  
- Temporary perturbation
- Long-term return to equilibrium

**Classification:**  
Temporal oversight

---

## Failure Mode 6 — Control Illusion

**Description:**  
Belief that extreme energy can be precisely controlled.

**Cause:**  
- Underestimating fluid chaos
- Overestimating directional coupling

**Effect:**  
- Unpredictable outcomes
- Massive collateral heating

**Classification:**  
Conceptual error

---

## Summary

> Cosmic Blast fails not subtly,  
> but violently and irreversibly.

Extreme force amplifies damage faster than it removes atmosphere.
